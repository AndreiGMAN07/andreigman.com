Recently I've finished one of the most important chapters in my life, Highschool. I have mixt feelings about it. On one hand I've grown up a lot, I've met up a lot of people, I've made some great friends, I've discovered lots of passions such as psychology, history, economics. I've learnt almost like a machine (not just academical stuff ^_^) especially this year, because I believe that I could be a better person every day. I've learnt to be a disciplined man, a self-taught, how to organise your time and how to react in critical moments, being able to make good choices as well. I've been trying to cheer people around me up, to have great time together and to "rise" like a forest.

On the other hand, I've been struggling to deal with emotions, stress, anxiety, depression (I had long periods of it). I've been betrayed by many, many people. I've always put others above me, because I believe in collectivity's power rather than individual one.

I've experienced all steps: success, growth, failure, decay and comfort zone. But I never gave up! Even though I wasn't a great man at all — as a human being I make mistakes, I learn, I move on and I teach others how to not repeat my mistakes. I am growing up constantly, day by day, month by month, year by year. Highschool was the transition between childhood and adult life. I hope that at University I'll meet up new people with different mentalities and I hope we'll help each other to improve. The human learns throughout his life. So, this is not the end — just a sunny beginning!
